[[app-K]]
= Chapter Source Notes and Citation Map

[abstract]
This appendix maps the principal claims, constructs, and methods in each chapter to the foundational and empirical sources collected in xref:app-I[Appendix I]. It provides a publication-ready citation plan without implying that every concept is established to the same degree. The map distinguishes mature foundations, practitioner syntheses, standards, and emerging research, and identifies where the book advances an original synthesis or proposes a research agenda.

== Citation conventions

The citation map follows several rules.

* Original papers and official standards are preferred over secondary summaries.
* A citation supports only the claim for which it is relevant; it does not endorse every conclusion in the source.
* Practitioner books are used for operating models and synthesis, not as substitutes for causal evidence.
* Preprints and recent AI studies are labeled as emerging evidence.
* The Engineering Change reference model, attention economics, integrated platform design, and several agent-governance concepts are original syntheses in this book and should not be presented as established external standards.
* Time-sensitive AI research and standards should be reverified before each publication edition.

== xref:ch-01[Chapter 1] — Introduction

=== Established foundations

The argument that developer productivity is multidimensional is supported by the SPACE framework and the DevEx framework. See <<forsgren2021-space>> and <<noda2023-devex>>.

The discussion of software delivery performance and organizational capability builds on the research synthesized in <<forsgren2018>> and the operating practices described in <<kim2021-devops>> and <<humble2010>>.

The warning against treating easily counted activity as productivity is consistent with measurement-validity principles in <<messick1995>>, indicator corruption in <<campbell1979>>, and Goodhart’s Law in <<goodhart1984>>.

The socio-technical framing is grounded in Conway’s organizational observation, socio-technical congruence, and distributed coordination research. See <<conway1968>>, <<cataldo2006>>, and <<herbsleb2007>>.

The discussion of human attention and interruption is informed by cognitive-load theory and interruption research. See <<sweller1988>> and <<mark2008>>.

The discussion of experimentation draws on the potential-outcomes framework, experimental design, and practical controlled-experiment methods. See <<rubin1974>>, <<holland1986>>, <<shadish2002>>, and <<kohavi2020>>.

=== Original synthesis

The Engineering Change as the principal unit of consequence, the explicit treatment of AI as an engineering participant, and the integration of attention, economics, architecture, causal inference, and governance form the book’s original synthesis.

== xref:ch-02[Chapter 2] — Engineering Economics

=== Established foundations

The distinction between technology adoption and realized productivity is supported by the productivity-paradox literature. See <<solow1987>>, <<brynjolfsson1993>>, and <<brynjolfsson2017>>.

The treatment of cost-benefit analysis, discounting, risk, and sensitivity follows the methods summarized in <<boardman2018>>.

The system-level emphasis on constraints and bottlenecks is informed by <<goldratt1984>> and the operational treatment of flow and capacity in <<hopp2011>>.

The distinction between gross local efficiency and end-to-end value is consistent with queueing and operations research, particularly <<little1961>>, <<kingman1961>>, and <<hopp2011>>.

=== Original synthesis

The capacity-realization factor, fragmentation discount, validation-burden accounting, and explicit taxonomy separating cash savings, avoided cost, released capacity, modeled outcome value, and option value are operational constructs developed for Engineering Intelligence.

== Citation integration roadmap

The next publication pass should:

. add inline cross-references from major empirical and methodological claims to the bibliography anchors;
. add source notes to figures whose structure derives from published frameworks;
. distinguish direct empirical support from analogy or synthesis;
. verify every DOI and official publication URL;
. remove or replace sources that cannot be verified from an authoritative publication record;
. add conflicting or null evidence where material;
. record verification dates for web-only standards and institutional publications;
. generate a citation-coverage report during the publication build.

== Citation map checklist

. Cite primary and official sources wherever available.
. Bound claims to the population and method of the cited evidence.
. Label practitioner synthesis, standards, and preprints accurately.
. Do not imply that a framework or standard proves empirical effectiveness.
. Identify original Engineering Intelligence concepts explicitly.
. Preserve unresolved questions and conflicting evidence.
. Reverify recent AI studies and standards before each edition.
. Treat citation coverage as a quality attribute of the publication.
