= Operating Templates and Records

[abstract]
This appendix provides reusable templates for running Engineering Intelligence as an operating discipline. The templates translate the concepts in this book into records that can be reviewed, versioned, audited, and reused across teams. They are intentionally concise enough for routine use while preserving the evidence, uncertainty, ownership, governance, and follow-up required for consequential decisions.

== How to use these templates

The templates may be implemented as YAML, JSON, database records, forms, or governed documents.

They should share several conventions:

* durable identifier;
* owner and accountable decision maker;
* semantic or document version;
* creation and review dates;
* evidence and metric references;
* explicit uncertainty;
* privacy and access classification;
* lifecycle status;
* correction history;
* expiration or next-review date.

== Evidence review agenda

A recurring evidence review should use the following agenda:

. decisions due before the next review;
. evidence requests and blocked questions;
. source and linkage quality exceptions;
. metric proposals, changes, and retirements;
. active experiments and guardrails;
. completed results and required decisions;
. value-realization variances;
. governance uses, exceptions, appeals, and incidents;
. architecture and platform risks;
. prior decision review dates.

The meeting should not become a general dashboard tour.

== Record status vocabulary

Recommended statuses are:

* draft;
* proposed;
* under review;
* approved;
* approved with conditions;
* active;
* degraded;
* suspended;
* superseded;
* deprecated;
* retired;
* rejected;
* completed.

Local systems may map these statuses, but should preserve the canonical meaning.

== Review-date discipline

Every decision, metric, model, governed use, exception, and business case should have a review date.

Review triggers may include:

* elapsed time;
* model or vendor version change;
* population shift;
* metric quality degradation;
* price or contract change;
* architecture migration;
* governance incident;
* guardrail breach;
* evidence contradicting the original assumption.

A record without a review condition tends to become permanent by default.

== Evidence references

References should use durable identifiers rather than presentation URLs alone.

Examples include:

* `metric://ei.review.validation_burden_minutes/1.0.0`;
* `study://exp_01J.../analysis/1.0.0`;
* `evidence://dataset/review_benchmark/2.1.0`;
* `decision://dec_01J...`;
* `model://change_risk_model/3.2.0`;
* `lineage://metric-run/01J...`;
* `business-case://bc_01J...`.

The resolver may redirect users to the current governed interface while preserving identity.

== Correction history

Records should not be silently overwritten.

A correction entry should contain:

[source,yaml]
----
correction:
  corrected_record: dec_01J...
  previous_version: 1.0.0
  new_version: 1.0.1
  field: evidence.metrics[0]
  previous_value: ei.review.validation_burden_minutes@0.9.0
  new_value: ei.review.validation_burden_minutes@1.0.0
  reason: final_validated_metric_version_was_used_in_analysis
  corrected_by: decision_steward
  corrected_at: 2026-10-16T09:00:00Z
----

== Minimum operating record set

An organization beginning Engineering Intelligence should implement at least:

. decision question;
. evidence request;
. metric specification and validation;
. data-quality exception;
. study charter and result;
. decision record;
. governed-use registration;
. correction request;
. business-case summary;
. value-realization record.

This set creates a traceable path from question to evidence, action, and realized outcome.

== Template anti-patterns

=== Form without ownership

The record is complete, but nobody is accountable for the decision or follow-up.

=== Required fields with meaningless placeholders

Users enter “N/A” merely to satisfy workflow rather than exposing missing evidence.

=== Record after the decision

A polished template is created retrospectively to justify a choice already made.

=== URLs instead of durable references

Evidence disappears when dashboards or documents move.

=== No review date

Temporary policy becomes permanent.

=== Templates as bureaucracy

Records are collected but never used in reviews, decisions, corrections, or learning.

== Operating-template checklist

. Assign durable identifiers and accountable owners.
. Start from the decision question rather than the available dashboard.
. Reference versioned metrics, models, studies, and evidence.
. State uncertainty, limitations, and prohibited uses.
. Record data quality and linkage exceptions.
. Separate forecast, modeled value, released capacity, and realized financial impact.
. Include conditions, reversal criteria, and review dates in decisions.
. Preserve correction and supersession history.
. Use registered purposes for access and governance.
. Retain negative results and stopped interventions.
. Review records through an operating cadence rather than storing them passively.
. Keep templates minimal enough to use and complete enough to support accountability.
