[[app-H]]
= Glossary and Controlled Vocabulary

[abstract]
This appendix defines the core vocabulary used throughout _Engineering Intelligence_. The purpose is not merely editorial consistency. Stable terminology is a prerequisite for valid metrics, interoperable data contracts, reproducible studies, auditable decisions, and trustworthy communication across engineering, product, finance, security, privacy, and executive leadership.

== Vocabulary principles

=== Prefer semantic meaning over tool terminology

Use terms such as _Engineering Change_, _validation run_, and _deployment_ when describing stable concepts. Preserve tool-specific terms such as pull request, workflow run, or release job as source-system implementations.

=== Distinguish observation from interpretation

An event is observed evidence. A reconstructed relationship is an inference. A prediction is a model output. A causal effect is an estimate under assumptions. A decision is an organizational choice.

=== Distinguish people, automation, and agents

Human, deterministic automation, AI assistant, and autonomous agent are separate actor types.

=== Distinguish assignment from exposure

A unit may be assigned to a treatment without receiving it.

=== Distinguish capacity from savings

Released engineering time is not automatically cash savings.

=== Version important semantics

Terms embedded in schemas, metrics, policies, or models should have stable identifiers and versioned definitions.

== A

=== Acceptance

The explicit or inferred decision that an artifact, finding, recommendation, or Engineering Change is suitable to proceed. Acceptance does not necessarily imply deployment, release, correctness, or realized value.

=== Acceptance rate

The proportion of eligible suggestions, findings, or artifacts accepted under a defined disposition rule. The denominator, partial acceptance, later rework, duplicates, and outcome window must be specified.

=== Accountable owner

The person or role responsible for ensuring a decision, metric, study, model, platform capability, or policy achieves its intended purpose and receives review.

=== Active time

Estimated time during which a person or system is substantively engaged in an activity. Active time differs from elapsed time and is often imperfectly observed.

=== Actor

A human, service account, deterministic automation, AI assistant, autonomous agent, vendor system, or organizational unit that initiates or performs an activity.

=== Agent

A software system that selects and performs actions toward a delegated objective, potentially using models, tools, memory, and other agents. An agent is distinguished from a single model invocation and from fixed automation.

=== Agentic workflow

A workflow in which one or more agents plan, delegate, execute, validate, or revise actions across multiple steps.

=== Aggregation

The process of combining observations across units, dimensions, or time. Valid aggregation depends on metric semantics; not all measures are additive.

=== Analysis unit

The unit represented by each analytical observation or error term. It may differ from the assignment unit in an experiment.

=== Annotation

A human- or machine-produced label applied to evidence for evaluation, training, classification, or adjudication.

=== Artifact

A created, modified, reviewed, executed, deployed, or released object, such as source code, configuration, test, package, image, schema, model, document, or deployment manifest.

=== Assignment

The allocation of an eligible unit to an experimental or policy variant. Assignment is not the same as treatment availability, invocation, completion, or use.

=== Assignment unit

The entity randomized or allocated to a treatment, such as an Engineering Change, person, repository, team, service, or time window.

=== Attention

Finite human cognitive capacity applied to understanding, deciding, creating, validating, coordinating, diagnosing, or recovering.

=== Attention cost

The opportunity cost of human attention consumed by an activity, queue, interruption, tool, or policy.

=== Attribution

The assignment of an outcome, cost, activity, or benefit to an intervention, actor, team, system, or Engineering Change. Attribution may be descriptive, probabilistic, causal, or accounting-based and should state which.

=== Audit trail

A durable record of evidence access, transformation, model or metric use, decisions, corrections, overrides, and automated actions.

=== Automation

A deterministic or rule-based system that performs predefined actions. Automation may use software logic without exercising agent-like planning or delegation.

=== Autonomous action

An action executed by a system without immediate human approval. Autonomy should be described by scope, consequence, reversibility, evidence threshold, and override controls.

=== Availability

The degree to which a system, treatment, service, or dataset is operationally accessible when required. Availability does not prove successful use.

== B

=== Backfill

Historical ingestion or recomputation used to populate evidence from a period before normal processing, restore missing data, or apply corrected logic.

=== Baseline

The pre-intervention or comparison state used to describe initial conditions, estimate expected outcomes, or improve analytical precision.

=== Benchmark

A governed set of tasks, examples, artifacts, labels, and evaluation rules used to compare tools, models, systems, or methods.

=== Benefit

A favorable incremental consequence relative to a credible alternative. Benefits may be cash impact, avoided cost, released capacity, quality improvement, risk reduction, option value, or capability value.

=== Blast radius

The set and severity of systems, users, data, dependencies, teams, or business outcomes potentially affected by a change or failure.

=== Blocking time

Elapsed time during which progress cannot continue because a required resource, decision, environment, dependency, or approval is unavailable.

=== Bot

An imprecise umbrella term for nonhuman activity. Canonical systems should instead identify deterministic automation, service account, AI assistant, or autonomous agent.

=== Bottleneck

The constrained stage or resource that limits end-to-end system throughput or responsiveness.

=== Build

The process or result of transforming source artifacts and dependencies into a deployable or distributable artifact.

=== Business case

A structured comparison of alternatives using incremental costs, benefits, uncertainty, risks, realization conditions, and decision criteria.

== C

=== Calibration

Agreement between predicted probability or confidence and observed frequency. A calibrated 70 percent prediction should be correct approximately 70 percent of the time in the relevant population.

=== Canonical event

A source-independent, versioned representation of an observed activity or state transition with preserved provenance.

=== Canonical identity

A stable identity that connects multiple source-system identities belonging to the same actor while preserving temporal validity and confidence.

=== Capacity

The amount of work or attention a system or resource can provide over a period under defined operating conditions.

=== Capacity realization

The proportion of released capacity that becomes valuable productive work, avoided cost, or financial impact.

=== Carryover

An effect of an earlier treatment period that persists into a later comparison period.

=== Causal claim

A claim that an intervention changed an outcome relative to the outcome that would otherwise have occurred.

=== Causal effect

A contrast between potential outcomes under different interventions for a defined population and estimand.

=== Change failure

A failure, degradation, rollback, incident, or material correction associated with a delivered Engineering Change under a specified attribution rule.

=== Change type

A governed classification of an Engineering Change, such as feature, defect fix, reliability, security, compliance, platform, experiment, migration, or maintenance.

=== Closed-loop automation

A system that observes, decides, acts, and evaluates outcomes repeatedly without requiring human approval for every cycle.

=== Cluster

A group assigned or analyzed together, such as a team, repository, service, or organization.

=== Cohort

A population grouped by a shared entry time, exposure, characteristic, or lifecycle condition.

=== Comparator

The alternative condition against which an intervention is evaluated.

=== Completeness

The degree to which expected evidence is present for the intended population and period.

=== Complexity

The cognitive, structural, behavioral, dependency, coordination, or operational difficulty associated with understanding and changing a system.

=== Confidence

A quantified or categorized estimate of how strongly evidence supports a relationship, classification, prediction, or conclusion. Confidence must identify its meaning and calibration.

=== Confidence band

A controlled category such as confirmed, high, medium, low, or rejected used to communicate relationship strength.

=== Confidence interval

A frequentist interval produced by a method that would contain the true parameter at the stated long-run rate under repeated sampling and model assumptions.

=== Confounder

A variable that influences both treatment or exposure and outcome, potentially producing a misleading association.

=== Consequence

The magnitude and type of impact resulting from an outcome, including human, technical, financial, regulatory, customer, or reputational effects.

=== Construct

An underlying concept the organization seeks to understand, such as review effort, maintainability, cognitive load, quality, or delivery responsiveness.

=== Construct validity

The degree to which a measure represents the intended construct rather than a related but different concept.

=== Contamination

Treatment received by a control unit or otherwise crossing the intended experiment boundary.

=== Continuity factor

An economic adjustment representing how much released time appears in usable blocks rather than fragmented moments.

=== Control group

The comparison group that does not receive the intervention of interest or receives the baseline condition.

=== Correction

A versioned change to erroneous or incomplete evidence, identity, linkage, metric, model output, or decision record that preserves prior state and reason.

=== Cost of delay

The value lost while an outcome, capability, risk reduction, or decision remains unavailable.

=== Counterfactual

The outcome that would have occurred under an alternative intervention or decision.

=== Credible interval

A Bayesian interval containing the parameter with a stated posterior probability under the model and prior assumptions.

=== Cycle time

Elapsed time between explicitly defined lifecycle events for a defined unit. The start, end, pauses, population, and calendar basis must be specified.

== D

=== Data contract

A versioned agreement defining source semantics, schemas, identifiers, timing, quality expectations, ownership, change policy, and permitted use.

=== Data lineage

Traceable relationships from a published result back through models, metrics, transformations, canonical records, and source evidence.

=== Data minimization

Collection, retention, and processing of only the evidence necessary for an approved purpose.

=== Data-quality gate

A rule that determines whether a metric, model, study, or decision product may be published or used.

=== Decision-grade

Sufficiently valid, reliable, timely, relevant, and governed for a specified consequential decision. Decision-grade is use-specific, not universal.

=== Decision owner

The accountable person or role authorized to choose among alternatives and responsible for reviewing the outcome.

=== Decision record

A durable statement of the question, alternatives, evidence, assumptions, uncertainty, chosen action, owner, conditions, and review date.

=== Delegation

The transfer of an objective, authority, or task from one actor to another.

=== Delegation graph

A representation of human and machine actors, delegated objectives, authority boundaries, actions, and acceptance responsibility.

=== Deployment

The movement or activation of one or more artifacts in an environment. Deployment is distinct from release to an intended population.

=== Deployment frequency

The number or rate of eligible deployments for a defined unit, environment, result, and period.

=== Descriptive claim

A claim about observed or reconstructed patterns without asserting prediction or causation.

=== Digital twin

A continuously updated computational representation of an engineering system used to simulate or estimate behavior under alternative conditions.

=== Direct cost

A cost traceable to the intervention, such as license, infrastructure, implementation, or support expense.

=== Distributional effect

Variation in benefit, burden, risk, or outcome across populations, subgroups, teams, or units.

=== Drift

A change over time in population, source semantics, feature distribution, model behavior, metric interpretation, treatment, or outcome relationship.

== E

=== Economic value

The incremental benefit of an intervention relative to a credible alternative after accounting for incremental cost, uncertainty, timing, and risk.

=== Effect size

The magnitude of a relationship or intervention effect, expressed in absolute, relative, standardized, or distributional terms.

=== Eligibility

The rules determining which units may enter a metric population, study, treatment, policy, or decision.

=== Engineering Change

A vendor-independent analytical unit representing an intended modification and its implementation, validation, delivery, operation, and observed outcomes.

=== Engineering Change graph

The entities and relationships connecting intent, actors, artifacts, activities, validations, builds, deployments, releases, incidents, and outcomes for Engineering Changes.

=== Engineering Intelligence

The discipline and supporting system for connecting engineering evidence, human attention, architecture, economics, experimentation, governance, and outcomes to improve organizational decisions and learning.

=== Estimand

The exact quantity a study intends to estimate, including population, treatment contrast, outcome, and handling of events such as noncompliance or missingness.

=== Event time

The time an event occurred in the source domain.

=== Evidence

Observed or derived information used to support a claim or decision. Evidence should identify source, provenance, quality, and interpretation level.

=== Evidence graph

A network linking questions, constructs, metrics, datasets, studies, results, decisions, and replications.

=== Evidence maturity

The strength and operational readiness of evidence, ranging from exploratory signal to replicated causal evidence and monitored policy.

=== Exposure

The treatment actually made available, invoked, completed, or used by an eligible unit.

=== External validity

The degree to which a result applies beyond the studied population, setting, intervention version, and period.

== F

=== Fairness

The evaluation and governance of whether benefits, burdens, errors, access, and consequences are acceptably distributed across relevant populations.

=== False negative

An eligible issue, outcome, or condition that exists but is not detected or classified as present.

=== False positive

A finding, prediction, or classification indicating an issue or condition that is not supported under the adjudication rule.

=== Feature

A measured or derived input used by a model, rule, analysis, or policy.

=== Finding

A specific concern, defect, recommendation, policy violation, or observation produced during review or analysis.

=== Finding acceptance

The disposition that a finding is sufficiently valid and actionable to warrant remediation, acknowledgment, or follow-up.

=== Finding recall

The proportion of eligible ground-truth findings detected by a reviewer or system.

=== Finding precision

The proportion of eligible produced findings judged valid and actionable.

=== Flow

The movement of Engineering Changes through planning, implementation, validation, delivery, and operation.

=== Fragmentation

The division of attention or released capacity into small intervals that reduce usability and increase switching cost.

=== Freshness

The delay between source occurrence and evidence or metric availability.

== G

=== Gaming

Behavior that improves a measured value without improving the intended construct or outcome.

=== Generated artifact

An artifact produced wholly or partly by an AI model or agent. Generated status does not imply quality or acceptance.

=== Generated explanation

A machine-produced narrative or summary derived from evidence or other model outputs. It should remain distinguishable from observed evidence.

=== Governance

The roles, policies, controls, reviews, audits, and accountability mechanisms governing evidence, metrics, models, experiments, automated actions, and decisions.

=== Governance incident

A material failure involving access, purpose limitation, prohibited use, privacy, metric misuse, unsafe automated action, or missing accountability.

=== Ground truth

A reference label or outcome treated as authoritative for an evaluation. Ground truth may itself contain uncertainty and should document adjudication.

=== Guardrail

A measure, threshold, or condition intended to detect unacceptable harm or degradation while optimizing another outcome.

== H

=== Hazard

The instantaneous rate at which an event occurs among units that have not yet experienced it.

=== Human-in-the-loop

A system in which a person participates in review, approval, correction, exception handling, or supervision. The phrase should specify the person’s actual authority and timing.

=== Human-on-the-loop

A system that acts autonomously within bounds while a person monitors, audits, or intervenes when necessary.

=== Human validation burden

The attention and effort required to establish sufficient confidence in machine- or human-produced output.

=== Hypothesis

A specific, falsifiable statement about a relationship, mechanism, or intervention effect.

== I

=== Identification strategy

The assumptions and design features allowing an observed contrast to be interpreted causally.

=== Identity resolution

The process of mapping source identities to canonical actors with temporal validity and confidence.

=== Immutable evidence

Source evidence preserved in a form that cannot be silently overwritten and can support replay, audit, or correction.

=== Implementation fidelity

The degree to which an intervention was delivered as designed.

=== Incident

An operational failure, degradation, security event, or significant near miss requiring coordinated response or investigation.

=== Incremental cost

The difference in cost between an intervention and the credible alternative.

=== Incremental value

The difference in value between an intervention and the credible alternative.

=== Inference

A conclusion, estimate, reconstruction, or prediction derived from evidence rather than directly observed.

=== Ingestion time

The time evidence entered the Engineering Intelligence platform.

=== Instrumentation

The technical and procedural mechanisms that create, collect, timestamp, and transmit evidence.

=== Instrumental variable

A variable used to estimate causal effects when it changes treatment, affects outcome only through treatment under assumptions, and satisfies other identification conditions.

=== Intention-to-treat effect

The effect of assignment to treatment regardless of whether treatment was received or completed.

=== Interference

A condition in which one unit’s treatment affects another unit’s outcome.

=== Internal validity

The credibility of a causal or analytical conclusion for the studied population and setting.

=== Intervention

A deliberate change to tool, workflow, policy, architecture, platform, staffing, or operating conditions intended to influence outcomes.

== J

=== Join coverage

The proportion of eligible records that can be linked across source or canonical entities under the declared rules.

== L

=== Latency

Elapsed time between defined events. The term should specify whether it refers to system response, queue delay, cycle time, or evidence freshness.

=== Learning organization

An organization that repeatedly observes, explains, intervenes, evaluates, decides, adapts, and preserves knowledge.

=== Learning velocity

The rate at which consequential uncertainty is reduced and translated into better decisions and retained knowledge.

=== Lifecycle state

A semantic state in the progress of an Engineering Change, such as proposed, active, validating, accepted, integrated, deployed, released, operating, completed, reverted, abandoned, or superseded.

=== Linkage confidence

The strength of evidence that two entities or events belong to the same Engineering Change or relationship.

=== Loaded labor cost

Compensation plus applicable benefits, payroll costs, facilities, equipment, management, and shared overhead used for economic modeling.

=== Longitudinal study

A study that observes units or populations across time to measure change, adaptation, retention, or delayed effects.

== M

=== Maintainability

The ease, safety, and cost with which a system can be understood, modified, validated, operated, and evolved.

=== Mechanism

The process through which an intervention or condition produces an outcome.

=== Measurement invariance

The degree to which a measure represents the same construct across groups, contexts, or time.

=== Measurement model

The explicit relationship between a construct and its observable indicators.

=== Metric

A versioned measure computed for a defined unit, population, interval, formula, and purpose.

=== Metric bundle

A coordinated set containing a primary outcome, guardrails, mechanism indicators, economic measures, adoption or exposure measures, and data-quality measures.

=== Metric incident

A material failure in metric correctness, timeliness, semantics, access, or permitted use.

=== Metric owner

The person or role accountable for a metric’s construct, definition, interpretation, quality, permitted use, review, and retirement.

=== Missingness

The absence of an expected value or relationship. Missingness should distinguish true zero, not applicable, unobserved, unavailable, unresolved, and not yet mature.

=== Model

A statistical, machine-learning, simulation, or rule-based representation used to predict, classify, rank, estimate, or recommend.

=== Model monoculture

Concentration on one model family, provider, or shared failure mode across consequential workflows.

=== Model provenance

The model identity, version, configuration, training or evaluation context, features, prompt, tools, and policy associated with an output.

=== Multi-tenancy

An architecture in which multiple organizational or regulatory tenants share some platform components while preserving defined isolation boundaries.

== N

=== Natural experiment

A situation in which external or operational variation creates a treatment contrast that can support causal inference under defensible assumptions.

=== Net attention change

Human attention reduced by an intervention minus validation, rework, coordination, and exception-handling attention added.

=== Net present value

The discounted value of future incremental benefits minus future incremental costs.

=== Noncompliance

A mismatch between assigned treatment and treatment actually received or followed.

=== Noninferiority

A study objective establishing that a treatment is not worse than a comparator by more than a predeclared acceptable margin.

=== Novelty

The degree to which a change introduces unfamiliar concepts, technologies, dependencies, patterns, or operating conditions.

== O

=== Observable

A value, event, artifact, or behavior that can be directly recorded or reconstructed with stated evidence.

=== Observability

The ability to infer relevant internal system state from available evidence. Engineering Intelligence observability includes data quality and semantic coverage, not only runtime telemetry.

=== Observational study

A study in which treatment or exposure is not assigned by the investigator.

=== Opportunity cost

The value of the best alternative use of money, attention, time, infrastructure, or organizational capacity.

=== Option value

The value created by preserving or enabling useful future choices under uncertainty.

=== Outcome

An observed consequence related to user value, business performance, quality, reliability, security, cost, capability, or learning.

=== Outcome maturity

The point at which sufficient time has elapsed for an outcome to be observed reliably.

=== Ownership

Responsibility for a system, artifact, metric, source, decision, or policy during a defined interval.

== P

=== Policy

A rule or decision framework governing eligibility, access, routing, approval, deployment, treatment, or action.

=== Policy-grade metric

A metric sufficiently mature for formal or automated policy use under continuous quality, monitoring, appeal, and rollback controls.

=== Population

The complete set of eligible units to which a metric, model, study, or conclusion applies.

=== Population drift

A change in the composition or characteristics of the eligible or observed population.

=== Precision

For finding or classification systems, the proportion of positive outputs that are valid under the adjudication rule.

=== Prediction

A model estimate of an unknown or future value, class, rank, or probability.

=== Predictive claim

A claim that available evidence can forecast an outcome for a defined population.

=== Prescriptive claim

A claim that one action should be selected over alternatives to optimize defined objectives under constraints.

=== Primary outcome

The predeclared outcome carrying the main confirmatory interpretation of a study.

=== Privacy

The appropriate control of personal or sensitive information, including collection, purpose, access, inference, retention, correction, and disclosure.

=== Probabilistic linkage

A relationship inferred from multiple uncertain signals rather than an explicit or deterministic reference.

=== Production function

The relationship between inputs such as labor, attention, tools, capital, and infrastructure and resulting engineering outcomes.

=== Prompt provenance

The identity and version of instructions, context policy, retrieved evidence, and configuration used for a model invocation.

=== Propensity score

The estimated probability of receiving treatment given observed pre-treatment characteristics.

=== Provenance

Information describing the origin, transformation, version, actor, time, and evidence chain of a record or result.

=== Proxy

An observable measure used to represent another construct. A proxy is not the construct itself.

=== Purpose limitation

The principle that evidence is collected and used only for declared, reviewed purposes.

== Q

=== Quality

Fitness of an Engineering Change or system for its intended purpose, including correctness, reliability, security, maintainability, usability, and compliance.

=== Quality gate

A condition that must be satisfied before an artifact, metric, model, treatment, or decision may proceed.

=== Queue

Work waiting for a constrained resource or process stage.

=== Queue delay

Elapsed time spent waiting before service begins.

=== Quasi-experiment

A nonrandomized study using a defensible identification strategy to estimate a causal effect.

== R

=== Randomization

Assignment by a chance mechanism designed to make treatment independent of potential outcomes within the target population.

=== Realized value

Benefit observed and verified after implementation. It should be labeled as booked financial impact, avoided cost, reallocated capacity, or observed outcome value.

=== Recall

For finding or classification systems, the proportion of eligible true cases detected.

=== Reconstruction

The derivation of entities, relationships, or lifecycle state from source evidence.

=== Registered use

An approved purpose-bound use of evidence, metrics, models, or automated policy with defined owner, population, access, controls, and review date.

=== Regression discontinuity

A causal design comparing units near a treatment-assignment threshold under continuity assumptions.

=== Reliability

The consistency and reproducibility of a measure, label, relationship, model, or process.

=== Release

The act of making a deployed change available to an intended user or consuming-system population.

=== Released capacity

Human or system capacity made available by an intervention. It is not automatically equivalent to savings or additional output.

=== Replay

Reprocessing preserved evidence through the same or revised transformations, reconstruction, metrics, or models.

=== Replication

Repeating or independently reproducing a study, measure, or finding to test robustness and boundary conditions.

=== Reproducibility

The ability to obtain the same result using the same evidence, code, versions, configuration, and analytical procedure.

=== Retention

The period and conditions under which evidence, identities, model outputs, metrics, and records are preserved.

=== Reversibility

The degree to which an intervention or automated action can be undone with limited cost or harm.

=== Review

A structured validation activity in which one or more actors evaluate an Engineering Change, artifact, finding, or decision.

=== Review effort

Human attention spent performing review. It should distinguish active effort from elapsed review time and queue delay.

=== Risk

The combination of uncertainty, probability, exposure, and consequence associated with an adverse outcome.

=== Risk class

A governed category representing expected consequence or control requirements for an Engineering Change or system.

=== Rollback

An action that removes, disables, or restores the effect of a deployment, release, policy, model, or metric version.

== S

=== Sample

The observed subset of a population used for measurement or inference.

=== Schema registry

A governed repository of versioned event, entity, and payload definitions with compatibility and ownership rules.

=== Sensitivity analysis

Evaluation of how conclusions change under alternative assumptions, parameters, populations, definitions, or missing-data mechanisms.

=== Service time

Time during which a resource actively processes a unit, excluding queue wait.

=== Shadow mode

Operation of a tool, model, metric, or policy without allowing it to affect the primary production decision, used for validation and comparison.

=== Source evidence

The original event, entity, payload, or state obtained from a source system before canonical interpretation.

=== Spillover

An effect of treatment on units other than those directly assigned or exposed.

=== Standard error

An estimate of the sampling variability of an estimator.

=== Statistical significance

A result meeting a predefined probability threshold under a statistical model. It does not imply practical importance, validity, causation, or economic value.

=== Stratification

Partitioning units into groups before assignment or analysis to improve balance, precision, or interpretation.

=== Study

A structured investigation designed to answer a descriptive, predictive, causal, or evaluative question.

=== Study charter

The record defining a study’s decision, ownership, hypothesis, population, intervention, outcomes, guardrails, duration, and governance.

=== Subgroup

A defined subset of a population used to evaluate heterogeneity, fairness, or boundary conditions.

=== Surveillance

Monitoring people or behavior in a way that exceeds a legitimate, proportionate, transparent, and governed purpose.

=== Synthetic control

A weighted combination of untreated units used to approximate the counterfactual outcome of a treated unit.

=== Synthetic evidence

Machine-generated, simulated, or transformed material that resembles observed evidence but must remain labeled as generated or inferred.

=== System of record

The authoritative source for a defined entity or fact. Authority may be split across domains and time.

== T

=== Technical debt

Future cost or constrained choice created by technical decisions that make change, validation, operation, or understanding more difficult.

=== Temporal join

A relationship resolved using values valid at the time of the event rather than current values.

=== Temporal ownership

Ownership represented as effective intervals so historical evidence is attributed to the correct team or role.

=== Treatment

The intervention condition assigned or delivered in a study or policy.

=== Treatment fidelity

The degree to which the actual treatment matches the declared configuration and operational behavior.

=== Treatment-on-treated effect

The estimated effect among units that actually received treatment, requiring assumptions beyond simple randomized assignment when noncompliance exists.

=== Throughput

The rate at which eligible units complete a defined process or lifecycle stage.

=== Tool usage

Observed invocation, interaction, or activity involving a tool. Usage is not proof of effectiveness or value.

=== Traceability

The ability to follow evidence, artifacts, activities, models, metrics, and decisions through their relationships and versions.

=== Transportability

The degree to which a causal result from one population or setting can be applied to another using evidence and assumptions.

== U

=== Uncertainty

Incomplete knowledge about evidence, measurement, model, relationship, outcome, effect, or future state.

=== Unit of analysis

See _analysis unit_.

=== Unit of consequence

The entity at which meaningful outcomes and risk are evaluated. In this framework, the Engineering Change is often the principal unit of consequence.

=== Utilization

The proportion of available service capacity currently consumed. High utilization can produce nonlinear queue growth.

== V

=== Validation

The process of establishing whether an artifact, finding, measure, model, intervention, or decision is sufficiently correct and fit for its intended use.

=== Validation burden

Human or system effort required to establish trust in output before acceptance or action.

=== Validation run

An execution that produces evidence about correctness, quality, safety, performance, compliance, or fitness.

=== Validity

The degree to which evidence, measurement, inference, or study supports the intended interpretation and use.

=== Value realization

The process by which forecast benefits become observed capacity, avoided cost, financial impact, risk reduction, or outcomes.

=== Vendor-neutral evidence

Evidence stored and defined independently of the vendor product that generated it, enabling comparison, audit, migration, and longitudinal analysis.

=== Version

A stable identifier for the semantics or implementation of a schema, metric, model, prompt, policy, treatment, study, or record.

== W

=== Wait time

Elapsed time during which a unit is ready but has not begun receiving the required service.

=== Work in progress

The number of units started but not completed within a defined lifecycle boundary.

=== Work mix

The distribution of change types, complexity, risk, domains, technologies, and other characteristics in a population.

=== Work-mix adjustment

Analytical control for differences in population composition when making comparisons.

=== Workflow

The activities, states, decisions, actors, and transitions through which an Engineering Change or related unit progresses.

== Controlled distinctions

=== Engineering Change versus pull request

An Engineering Change is the semantic unit of intended consequence. A pull request is one possible implementation artifact or review container.

=== Deployment versus release

A deployment places artifacts into an environment. A release exposes functionality to an intended population.

=== Activity versus outcome

Activity records work performed. Outcome records consequence or value.

=== Elapsed time versus active effort

Elapsed time includes waiting and inactivity. Active effort attempts to represent substantive attention or processing.

=== Usage versus effectiveness

Usage establishes interaction. Effectiveness establishes improvement in a defined outcome.

=== Adoption versus exposure

Adoption describes broader use or acceptance of a tool or practice. Exposure records whether a specific unit received a treatment.

=== Correlation versus causation

Correlation describes association. Causation claims an intervention changed the outcome under an identification strategy.

=== Prediction versus causal estimate

Prediction estimates an unknown outcome. A causal estimate compares potential outcomes under interventions.

=== Confidence versus probability

Confidence may refer to relationship strength, model score, or interval procedure. Probability must identify the random event or posterior statement.

=== Precision versus accuracy

Precision in information retrieval is the proportion of positive findings that are valid. Accuracy is the proportion of all classifications that are correct and can be misleading with class imbalance.

=== Capacity value versus cash savings

Capacity value estimates potential economic usefulness of released time. Cash savings require an actual or committed financial change.

=== Avoided cost versus savings

Avoided cost prevents a planned future expenditure. Savings reduce an existing or committed expense.

=== Generated explanation versus observed evidence

A generated explanation interprets evidence. It is not itself the original event or outcome.

=== Automation versus agent

Automation follows predefined logic. An agent selects and sequences actions toward a delegated objective.

=== Review finding versus defect

A review finding is an assertion requiring disposition. A defect is a validated undesirable condition.

=== Quality signal versus quality outcome

A signal is an indicator such as test failure or review finding. An outcome is an observed consequence such as escaped defect, rollback, or user impact.

=== Metric quality versus engineering quality

Metric quality describes whether measurement is trustworthy. Engineering quality describes the fitness of the system or change.

=== Data freshness versus event latency

Freshness is delay before evidence becomes available. Event latency may describe operational processing or user-facing response time.

=== Governance approval versus scientific validity

Approval authorizes a use under controls. It does not establish that the metric, model, or causal claim is scientifically valid.

=== Reproducibility versus replication

Reproducibility reruns the same method and evidence. Replication tests the result in new data, populations, periods, or implementations.

=== Internal validity versus external validity

Internal validity concerns correctness for the studied setting. External validity concerns generalization beyond it.

=== Policy versus treatment

A policy is an ongoing decision rule. A treatment is a condition evaluated or delivered to eligible units.

=== Guardrail versus target

A target is an outcome to improve. A guardrail defines unacceptable degradation or harm.

=== Correction versus deletion

Correction preserves and amends history. Deletion removes evidence according to legal or policy requirements.

== Vocabulary governance

Terms used in canonical schemas, metric specifications, model registries, experiment protocols, and governance records should be governed through:

* stable term identifier;
* preferred label;
* definition;
* permitted synonyms;
* deprecated terms;
* owner;
* semantic version;
* affected schemas and products;
* effective date;

== Vocabulary checklist

. Use stable semantic terms rather than source-vendor objects where possible.
. Define every metric term independently of its current implementation.
. Distinguish observed evidence, reconstruction, prediction, causal estimate, and decision.
. Distinguish human, automation, assistant, and autonomous-agent activity.
. Distinguish assignment, availability, invocation, exposure, adoption, and outcome.
. Distinguish elapsed time, queue delay, service time, and active effort.
. Distinguish released capacity, avoided cost, savings, and realized value.
. Version definitions used in schemas, metrics, models, policies, and records.
. Deprecate ambiguous terms rather than silently changing their meaning.
. Review vocabulary when tools, workflows, architecture, or organizational practice changes.
