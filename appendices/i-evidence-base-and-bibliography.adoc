= Evidence Base and Bibliography

[abstract]
This appendix provides a curated evidence base for the concepts developed throughout _Engineering Intelligence_. It includes foundational research in software delivery, developer productivity, socio-technical systems, causal inference, measurement, queueing, organizational learning, AI evaluation, governance, and engineering economics. The bibliography is not intended to imply that every claim in this book has already been settled. It identifies the intellectual foundations, established methods, and important areas of active research that support or challenge the framework.

== How to use this evidence base

The sources are grouped by purpose rather than alphabetically.

They can be used to:

* support chapter-level citations;
* design metric and experiment standards;
* distinguish established results from emerging claims;
* identify original methods rather than secondary summaries;
* guide further reading and replication;
* maintain a versioned source registry for the book.

Where possible, entries include a DOI, official publication page, standards body, or publisher reference.

[[martin2017]]

== Source-evidence classification

The book uses the following evidence categories:

=== Foundational theory

Long-standing conceptual or mathematical work, such as measurement scales, causal inference, modularity, queueing, or organizational learning.

=== Peer-reviewed empirical evidence

Published studies with explicit data, methods, and review.

=== Standards and official frameworks

Normative or advisory publications from recognized standards bodies and public institutions.

=== Practitioner synthesis

Books and articles integrating research and field practice.

=== Emerging preprint

Current research that may be useful but has not necessarily completed peer review or replication.

=== Organizational case evidence

Evidence from one company, tool, or operating context. Such evidence may be highly relevant but should not be assumed universally transportable.

== Citation-quality rules

When adding citations to the body of the book:

. cite the original source where available;
. distinguish peer-reviewed work from preprints and practitioner material;
. avoid treating standards as proof of empirical effectiveness;
. avoid using one study to support a universal claim;
. include study population and setting when material;
. cite methods separately from substantive findings;
. identify sources with conflicts of interest or vendor sponsorship;
. preserve negative or conflicting evidence;
. review time-sensitive sources and standards before publication;
. use stable citation keys and durable publication identifiers.

== Evidence gaps and active research areas

Important areas remain unsettled or underdeveloped:

* long-term effects of AI assistance on engineering skill formation;
* causal effects of AI tools on production quality and business outcomes;
* reliable measurement of active cognitive effort;
* transportability of developer-productivity studies across organizations;
* architecture metrics with strong construct and predictive validity;
* economic realization of released engineering capacity;
* evaluation of multi-agent software-engineering systems;
* correlated failure and model-monoculture risk;
* privacy-preserving cross-organization benchmarks;
* governance mechanisms that protect trust without blocking useful learning;
* digital twins and prescriptive policies for engineering organizations.

These gaps should be treated as a research agenda rather than filled with unsupported certainty.

== Bibliography maintenance checklist

. Prefer original papers, official standards, and authoritative editions.
. Store stable citation keys and publication identifiers.
. Label preprints and emerging evidence clearly.
. Review standards and recent AI research before each edition.
. Add citations to chapter claims rather than leaving references only in this appendix.
. Include evidence that challenges the preferred interpretation.
. Preserve historical editions when definitions or standards change.
. Record retrieval or verification dates for web-only sources.
. Avoid vendor marketing as the sole source for effectiveness claims.
. Treat the bibliography as a governed, versioned part of the Engineering Intelligence evidence system.
