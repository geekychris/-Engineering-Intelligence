# Chapter opening illustration prompts

Prompts for generating per-chapter opening plates that harmonize with the front
cover. Use each one as-is in an image-generation tool (Midjourney, Sora,
DALL·E 3, Stable Diffusion XL, Imagen); the shared style block is prepended so
the plates read as a set rather than 18 unrelated pictures.

**Target format:** landscape banner roughly 6.5 × 2.6 inches (≈ 3:1). Leave
the lower band clear of detail so the chapter title can sit over it without a
busy background. No text or lettering inside the image.

**Naming convention:** save each result as
`figures/plates/chapter-01.png` … `chapter-18.png`. To use them, edit
`scripts/build.sh`'s `render_chapter_plates` to select `chapter-<N>.png` per
chapter file instead of the procedural ornament.

---

## Shared style block (paste on every prompt)

> Illustrated book chapter opening plate in the style of a warm, atmospheric
> technical-book cover. Painterly landscape softly fused with clean blueprint
> linework and a few physical study objects (open notebook, brass instruments,
> stacked hardcovers). Golden-hour lighting; matte, slightly nostalgic, like a
> vintage engineering frontispiece. Palette anchored on deep navy #17365D,
> warm cream #EEE2C8, brass gold #B78331, sepia #6C371F, muted sky blue #8FC0E8,
> and forest green. Composition: horizontal plate, ~3:1 aspect ratio, quiet
> lower band left uncluttered so a chapter title could sit centred over it.
> No text, no captions, no visible words anywhere in the image.

## Chapter 1 — Introduction

Wide painterly view of a small research desk at dawn beside a large open
window that looks onto a valley with a bridge, a river, and a distant city
skyline. On the desk: an open notebook with hand-drawn diagrams of
interconnected nodes, a lit brass lamp, a stack of well-worn hardcovers, a
compass, and a coffee cup faintly steaming. Blueprint linework drifts over
the sky like translucent tracing paper. The scene reads as the moment of
setting out on a long study — quiet, purposeful, and inviting.

## Chapter 2 — Engineering Economics

Painterly still life on a wooden drafting table: a brass balance scale in
sharp focus, one pan holding coins and a small stack of receipts, the other
pan holding an hourglass and a folded blueprint. Around the scale, a
notebook is open to a ledger of hand-written figures with tidy columns and
an underlined total. A distant, slightly blurred backdrop shows a bridge
half-drawn as construction blueprint and half-rendered as a real structure
at golden hour — the transformation of specification into cost.

## Chapter 3 — Engineering Change

An open leather-bound notebook centred on a drafting desk, its pages filled
with an intricate hand-drawn graph — nodes and edges connecting labelled
boxes for _intent_, _artifact_, _review_, _deploy_, _outcome_ (drawn as
symbols, not words). Overlaid faintly across the same view: a delicate
blueprint of the same graph, rotated slightly, hinting at a formal
representation. On one side of the notebook rests a set of brass calipers
and a small ink pen. Warm cream paper glow.

## Chapter 4 — Human Attention

A softly lit study desk photographed slightly from above at dusk: a single
open book at the centre held down by a paperweight, a half-drunk coffee cup
to the side, a dimmed brass reading lamp, and a small hourglass whose sand
is nearly run out. Behind the desk, translucent overlays hint at intrusions
— a phone screen glowing, a sticky note lifting on a breeze, a distant open
door casting a long light. The composition centres focus on the book while
subtly showing the pull of distractions.

## Chapter 5 — Measuring Engineering Systems

Painterly rendering of a set of concentric rings drawn on cream paper,
suggesting a target or telescope reticle, superimposed over a landscape of
mountains and a slow river below at first light. In the foreground on a
desk: a brass sextant, a folded map, and a precision ruler. Delicate
blueprint tick-marks and unit annotations float around the rings. The image
should feel like the moment of taking a reading — measured, careful, and
observational rather than exploratory.

## Chapter 6 — Experimentation and Causal Inference

Two nearly identical desks shown side by side at golden hour, one bathed in
cool light and the other in warm light, connected by a delicate arc of
graph paper suggesting a controlled comparison. On each desk: an open
notebook with a small hand-drawn directed graph (nodes and arrows). Between
them, a brass balance and a translucent overlay of a bell curve, suggested
rather than fully drawn. In the background a bridge under construction with
one half painted and the other still in blueprint — parallel worlds under
comparison.

## Chapter 7 — AI-Assisted Engineering

A shared desk seen at slightly elevated angle: a leather-bound notebook and
a brass pen on the left, and on the right a softly glowing tablet or slate
displaying a delicately drawn abstract circuit pattern in gold and navy —
not photorealistic tech, but something between an illuminated manuscript
and a printed circuit. A translucent hand reaches toward a page from one
side, and a delicate line of light bridges the notebook and the slate.
Warm, collaborative mood.

## Chapter 8 — Enterprise Instrumentation

A cutaway painterly view showing an old-fashioned observatory or engine
room repurposed as a data infrastructure: brass pipes and valves along one
wall carry glowing filaments of light down into a receiving vessel, above
which floats a translucent blueprint of concentric event streams. In the
foreground on a workbench: a stack of hardcovers, a schematic pinned to a
board, and an open toolbox with precision instruments. The scene evokes
disciplined instrumentation — measured, engineered, plumbed.

## Chapter 9 — Complexity and Blast Radius

A wide painterly landscape looking down over a fictional city built on a
mountain valley at dusk: rope bridges connect districts in intricate
patterns. A single lit lantern is shown mid-swing on one bridge; its light
extends outward as translucent concentric rings that touch multiple distant
districts. In the foreground, a hand-drawn dependency graph is faintly
overlaid on the sky. The image conveys how one small motion can propagate
outward through a connected system.

## Chapter 10 — Engineering Intelligence Platform

Painterly cutaway of a classical library-observatory hybrid: a great domed
room seen in three-quarter view with tiered mezzanines full of books and a
central brass mechanism resembling an orrery. The orrery's arms carry
labelled glass phials — each containing a differently colored fluid — that
feed a central vessel from which a single beam of light rises. A rolled
blueprint sits on the foreground desk. The architecture reads as durable,
governed, and purposeful — a platform, not a dashboard.

## Chapter 11 — Engineering Metrics Catalogue

A librarian's high shelf viewed slightly from below at golden hour: rows of
identical brass-lettered spines organized precisely by category, each
spine adorned with a small emblem (a scale, a magnifying glass, a compass,
a gear, a flask). On the foreground table in soft focus: a card catalog
drawer left open, an ink pen resting across a filled index card, and a
notebook page with hand-written cross-references. The scene evokes a
governed catalogue rather than a data warehouse.

## Chapter 12 — Experiment Playbooks

An open field-notebook on a workbench, its pages divided into hand-drawn
recipe-like panels — each with a small icon, a numbered sequence, and a
short marginal note (words abstracted, not readable). Beside the notebook:
a small tray with prepared instruments (a stopwatch, a set of coloured
tokens, a pipette). In the background, a chalkboard bears a lightly sketched
protocol diagram. The mood is that of an experimental cookbook: everything
laid out to be executed the same way twice.

## Chapter 13 — Governance, Ethics, and Adoption

A quiet council-chamber scene at dusk: a round oak table with three empty
chairs, a single lit candle in a brass holder at centre, and a folded
document sealed with wax to one side. Softly translucent, in the room's
upper air: overlapping profiles of faces suggesting deliberation but no
identifiable individuals. In the background, a tall window frames a
distant, lit city — the community being served. The image conveys careful
stewardship, boundaries, and legitimacy rather than surveillance.

## Chapter 14 — Portfolio and Executive Decision-Making

An elevated map-table lit from above, showing a stylized painted map of a
fictional archipelago at dusk. Small brass tokens are positioned on
selected islands, and delicate lines drawn between them suggest options,
dependencies, and shipping routes. To one side of the table sits a rolled
scroll, a compass, and a ledger with columns of numbers. Overhead in soft
focus: a hint of a large window looking out on a coastal city. The mood is
that of allocated attention — a captain reading a chart before ordering.

## Chapter 15 — Implementation and Rollout

Painterly diagonal view of a bridge under construction seen at early
morning, with three completed spans and two still in staging: cranes hold
segments in place, workers pace scaffolding, and blueprint sheets flap in
the breeze near the near-side abutment. On a small worktable in the
foreground: a plan, a mug, a hard hat, and a set of surveying stakes. The
image reads as measured, phased, careful — building rather than launching.

## Chapter 16 — The Science of Engineering Intelligence

A quiet laboratory reading room: a long wooden bench facing a large
window, on the bench a microscope, a set of neatly filed research folders,
a printed dataset held down by a small brass paperweight, and an open
peer-reviewed journal (words abstracted). Behind the bench, a chalkboard
carries a partly-worked derivation and a small hand-drawn power curve.
Through the window: a courtyard where two figures stand talking, hinting
at replication and community. Warm scholarly light.

## Chapter 17 — The Learning Engineering Organization

An interior painterly scene of a warm, well-lit library at evening, where
a small group of people (rendered softly, not detailed) sit around a
central round table strewn with notebooks and open volumes. A brass
telescope sits on a stand nearby, pointed inward at the group — the
organization observing itself. Overlaid faintly across the ceiling: a
delicate circular arrow suggesting a learning loop. The mood is
collaborative deliberation, retrospection, and calm intent.

## Chapter 18 — The Future of Engineering Intelligence

A tall painterly landscape of a shoreline at dawn: a brass telescope on a
tripod overlooks a bay in which fishing boats, mail boats, and a couple of
sleek half-visible autonomous vessels share the water peacefully. The
horizon is soft, with the outline of a distant new city partly finished
in blueprint linework and partly rendered as buildings under actual light.
The image conveys anticipation, plurality of agents, and continued
navigation rather than technological rupture.

## After you generate them

1. Save each PNG at ~1600×640 with a transparent or cream background.
2. Drop the files into `figures/plates/` as `chapter-01.png` … `chapter-18.png`.
3. Update `scripts/build.sh`'s `render_chapter_plates` to prefer
   `figures/plates/chapter-<NN>.png` when present, falling back to the
   procedural ornament otherwise. I can wire that swap for you.
