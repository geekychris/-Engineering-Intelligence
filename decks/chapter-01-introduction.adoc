== Why we need a new discipline

Modern engineering orgs are drowning in operational data — commits, builds, deploys, incidents, AI suggestions — yet still cannot answer:

* What does an engineering change actually cost?
* Where is scarce expert attention consumed?
* Which review activities prevent defects; which merely add delay?
* Does an AI reviewer reduce burden, or create a new one?

[.lead]
The obstacle is not a lack of metrics. It is a lack of _models_.

// ============================================================================

== From metric to model

A *metric* describes an observation.
A *model* explains how observations relate, supports prediction, and lets rival interventions be compared.

Lead time is useful as a description. It becomes _operationally_ valuable only when you can:

* separate waiting from active work
* control for change complexity
* identify the constrained stage
* estimate an intervention's counterfactual impact

// ============================================================================

== The change in objective

[quote]
____
The purpose of engineering measurement is not to count activity.
It is to improve decisions about how scarce engineering resources are used.
____

The scarcest resource in most engineering organizations is not compute or money — it is *expert human attention*.

// ============================================================================

== From dashboards to a learning system

image::figures/mermaid/engineering-intelligence-loop.png[The Engineering Intelligence learning loop]

Six capabilities close the loop: *Observation → Reconstruction → Modeling → Explanation → Experimentation → Learning*.

Measurement that does not change a decision is, at best, descriptive.

// ============================================================================

== The limits of familiar productivity measures

* Lines of code, commit count, PR count, story points, utilization — all _local, gameable, context-dependent_
* DORA metrics and SPACE are system-level foundations, not universal scores
* Engineering Intelligence _extends_ them with:
** a richer unit of analysis than the individual PR
** explicit economic and cognitive models
** first-class treatment of AI participants
** an experimentation and causal-inference layer

// ============================================================================

== The Engineering Change

The pull request is a platform artifact. It is not the unit of engineering work.

[IMPORTANT]
====
An *Engineering Change* is the set of human, AI, code, configuration, data, infrastructure, review, verification, deployment, and operational activities required to move one coherent engineering intent from conception to an accepted outcome.
====

One product change may span 8+ PRs, migrations, flags, approvals, deployments — or one PR may bundle several unrelated intents.

// ============================================================================

== The change graph

image::figures/mermaid/engineering-change-graph.png[Engineering Change graph]

* Intent → many PRs, deploys, approvals
* Attribution runs on the _whole graph_, not the artifact
* Reconstruction may be explicit (change ID, plan) or probabilistic
* Confidence and provenance must be preserved

== Engineering is a socio-technical system

A two-line config change can be _trivial_ in code and _catastrophic_ in blast radius.
A 10,000-line generated client can be _large_ and _low-risk_.

Change is a *multidimensional vector*:

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Implementation | control flow, concurrency, language difficulty
| Domain         | security, distributed systems, regulation
| Architectural  | services, dependency depth, contracts
| Verification   | test environments, nondeterminism
| Deployment     | ordering, migrations, rollback paths
| Organizational | teams, approvals, decision rights
| Blast radius   | users, data, money, availability, compliance
| Novelty        | distance from patterns already understood
|===

// ============================================================================

== Human attention as an economic resource

An hour of uninterrupted work is not equivalent to twelve five-minute interruptions.

Four kinds of human effort — all real, only some visible:

. *Production* — code, designs, tests, docs
. *Verification* — review, validate, challenge
. *Coordination* — align people, schedules, decisions
. *Recovery* — reconstruct context after interruption

Recovery is usually invisible in cost accounting — and usually the largest hidden number.

// ============================================================================

== AI as a first-class participant

An AI reviewer is not a software license. It participates in engineering work.

* *Cognitive offload* — reasoning avoided because a reliable AI contribution performed it first
* *Cognitive reload* — extra human work created by noisy or untrustworthy AI output

[.lead]
Acceptance rate is not evidence of offload. The correct object of study is the causal effect on quality-adjusted outcomes.

// ============================================================================

== Six questions for every metric

. *Purpose* — what decision does it inform?
. *Definition* — what is counted, at what unit?
. *Instrumentation* — which events and entities are required?
. *Interpretation* — what mechanisms move it?
. *Failure modes* — how can it be biased or gamed?
. *Actionability* — what intervention becomes possible?

Metrics without a decision or an owner _accumulate cost while adding noise_.

// ============================================================================

== Experimentation as infrastructure

Before-and-after averages mis-attribute. Adoption is non-random. Novelty decays. Definitions drift.

A credible evaluation specifies:

* intervention and mechanism
* assignment vs. analysis unit
* primary and guardrail outcomes
* compliance, interference, stopping rules
* heterogeneous effects and external events

Randomised experiments _when feasible_; staggered rollouts, diff-in-diff, synthetic controls _when not_.

== Key takeaways

* Organizations lack _models_, not metrics
* The *Engineering Change* — not the PR — is the primary unit
* *Attention* is the scarce economic resource
* *AI* is a participant that creates both offload and reload
* Metrics exist to support _decisions_, not dashboards
* Engineering Intelligence is a closed loop from observation to learning
