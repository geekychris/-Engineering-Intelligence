== Where we are today

* Engineering organisations are richly _instrumented_ but poorly _understood_
* Dashboards proliferate; decisions don't get better
* Every leader has "the number" — nobody agrees on what it means
* AI has raised the stakes without providing an answer to _"is it helping?"_

[.lead]
Engineering Intelligence is a discipline that answers those questions with evidence, not opinion.

// ============================================================================

== The five questions this book answers

. What does an Engineering Change actually cost?
. Where is scarce human attention consumed?
. Which review activities prevent defects, and which merely add delay?
. Does an AI-assisted workflow genuinely improve outcomes?
. Which portfolio investment reduces the most system risk per dollar?

// ============================================================================

== The central argument

[quote]
Software engineering is a measurable socio-technical system.
Its performance can be modeled, measured, and improved — but only if
we treat evidence, inference, and decision as separable objects with
explicit governance.

// ============================================================================

== The unit of analysis: the Engineering Change

The pull request is a platform artifact. It is not the unit of engineering work.

* One capability = many pull requests, deployments, approvals, feature flags
* One pull request may contain unrelated intents
* Reconstructing the *Engineering Change* is the first step to any credible metric

// ============================================================================

== Engineering as a queueing system

Delay is not linear in load — it explodes near saturation.

image::../build/figures/mermaid/context-switch-cycle.png[width=70%]

Managing utilisation is a first-class economic problem, not a scheduling detail.

// ============================================================================

== Attention is the constraint

* Compute can be provisioned. Attention cannot.
* Cognitive load is multidimensional: intrinsic, representational, navigational, coordination, temporal, verification, tool interaction.
* An 8-person team paying 3.4 context-switches × 12 min × 230 days ≈ *$174k/year* of pure recovery overhead.
* A modest consolidation intervention saves *$73k/year* — an economic argument, not a preference.

== Measurement hierarchy

[cols="1,3"]
|===
| Events | pull-request created, review submitted, deployment recorded
| Observations | normalised facts derived from events
| Features | explanatory variables (dependency depth, ownership boundary)
| Metrics | summarised quantities that answer a *decision question*
| Models | relationships used to explain, predict, or counterfactually estimate
| Decisions | actions taken because of the evidence
|===

Confusing these layers is the leading cause of dashboard proliferation.

// ============================================================================

== A good metric ...

* Ties to a *decision*
* Measures the *construct* it claims to measure
* Is reliable, sensitive, robust
* Has *governance*: version, uncertainty, owner, guardrails
* Has a documented *retirement* criterion

Metrics without decision, owner, or retirement _accumulate cost while adding noise_.

// ============================================================================

== Causal reasoning, not correlation

Before-and-after averages mislead. The DAG makes assumptions explicit.

image::../figures/plots/causal-dag.png[width=70%]

Randomisation cuts the confounding path. Adjustment blocks it. Neither happens by accident.

// ============================================================================

== The naive "AI reviewer worked!" mistake

[cols="1,1,1,1"]
|===
| Analysis | Estimate | Says what | Right for
| Naive before-after | −1.9h | Least reliable | Nothing serious
| Difference-in-differences | −1.1h | Effect of _offering_ the tool | Mandate decisions
| Complier-average (CACE) | −1.55h | Effect _among users_ | Voluntary adoption
|===

Reporting the wrong estimand mis-prices the rollout.

// ============================================================================

== AI as a first-class participant

* AI creates *cognitive offload* AND *cognitive reload*.
* Net Cognitive Effect: latexmath:[$NCE = W_{offload} - W_{reload}$]
* Quality-adjusted: multiply by retention factor to catch quality regression
* An AI reviewer can save 3.3 min direct review time while adding 2.9 min of triage and rework — net 0.4 min saved, not the headline 19%

// ============================================================================

== Blast radius is a structural property

* Number of downstream services affected
* Fraction that are customer-facing
* Difficulty of coordinated rollback

A 38-line "small" change can score in the *top quartile* of blast radius. The point of the score is not to block — it is to select the right rollout playbook.

== Governance is not paperwork

Every use is classified 0–4 by consequence and sensitivity.

[cols="1,2,2"]
|===
| Class | Example | Review body
| 0 | Platform operations | Lightweight
| 1 | Team retrospectives | Lightweight
| 2 | Portfolio decisions | Standard
| 3 | Policy influence | Standard
| 4 | Individual consequence | Full governance board (usually prohibited)
|===

Misclassification is more common — and more dangerous — than the wrong statistical test.

== Portfolio: what "the math" cannot decide

Three initiatives compete for 42 general + 6 specialist engineer-quarters.

* Initiative A: $8M value, 24+3 quarters
* Initiative B: $4M value, 30+0 quarters
* Initiative C: *Regulatory* — mandatory, 18+5 quarters, deadline hard

C is a floor, not a choice. A's monthly cost of delay is the price of unlocking C.

That trade-off is legible when reported as a bundle, invisible under an ROI score.

== What we recommend

Start with:

. One consequential decision — not universal instrumentation
. Ingest sources for that decision only
. Build governed metric v1.0 with uncertainty and guardrails
. Register the use and its expected action
. Iterate; publish negative results alongside positive ones

*Ninety days.* Not two years.

// ============================================================================

== What success looks like

* Every executive metric has a decision owner and a review date
* Analyses cite a metric _version_
* AI adoption produces both a QCOI number _and_ a guardrail report
* Individual engineers are never ranked by system telemetry
* Retrospectives show the *ratio of decisions changed* by evidence rising quarter-over-quarter
